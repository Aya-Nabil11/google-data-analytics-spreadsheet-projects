## ğŸ§  Key Concepts Covered

### Structured Thinking
A systematic approach to problem-solving that involves clearly defining the problem, organizing available information, identifying gaps, and planning analysis steps before starting work.

### Scope of Work (SOW)
A formal document that outlines what work will be done in a project, including deliverables, timelines, milestones, and reporting expectations. It ensures alignment between analysts, stakeholders, and sponsors.

---

## ğŸ§© Components of the Scope of Work

### Deliverables
Clearly defined outputs expected from the project, such as cleaned datasets, analysis reports, or visualizations. Deliverables are specific, measurable, and tied to business goals.

### Milestones
Key checkpoints that indicate progress throughout the project. Milestones help track whether the project is on schedule and whether major steps have been completed.

### Timeline
A structured schedule that maps tasks and milestones over time. The timeline sets expectations for when each part of the project will be completed.

### Reports
Planned updates shared with stakeholders to communicate progress, findings, and completion of milestones. Reports help maintain transparency and alignment.
